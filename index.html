05
Campaign · Product Launch

Mit gældsoverblik — Product Launch for Danish Businesses

The Danish Debt Collection Agency (Gældsstyrelsen)

Concept, art direction, and end-to-end production of a LinkedIn launch campaign for Mit gældsoverblik — the national platform where Danish citizens and companies can see all their public debts in one place. Aimed at the business segment (erhverv), the creative shows small business owners stunned into silence as paper debts pile in their hands — the moment before they discover the tool that finally makes sense of it all.

To build a consistent audience universe, I built the campaign's personas myself: sourcing real people from Getty Images, then re-styling their wardrobe and re-lighting them to become the target-group archetypes (the consultant and the carpenter). The same visual language then carried across the launch banner, the two hero films, and the wider paid rollout — one integrated system, not a set of adapted assets.

About

Three languages. Twenty years of practice. One fixed conviction.

Good communication is invisible. The moment the audience notices the craft, the message has already lost. That conviction has shaped every project I've worked on — from McCann Erickson in Santiago to the Danish Debt Collection Agency (Gældsstyrelsen) in Denmark.

Today I run the full visual production pipeline at the Danish Debt Collection Agency: from strategy and scripting through studio shoots, motion design, and final delivery. What's distinctive is how AI fits in — not as a shortcut, but as production capacity. I work with language models the way a producer works with a team: clear briefs, strong editorial judgment, deliberate control of quality.

The result is a lean operation that delivers at a level that once required a full team.
